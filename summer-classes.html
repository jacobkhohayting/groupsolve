Summer Classes

GroupSolve’s summer classes are designed to help students build strong mathematical thinking through discovery, exploration, and problem-solving instead of memorizing formulas without understanding.

Ask a question Back to schedule

Learn math by discovering it

Our classes focus on helping students understand why math works. Through guided problems, discussions, and interactive lessons, students are encouraged to make connections, test ideas, and build confidence.

Class levels

Students will be placed into a level based on their background, comfort with math, and diagnostic results.

01

Introductory Math

This class is designed for elementary school students who are ready to strengthen their foundations and explore math in a deeper, more creative way.

  • Number sense and patterns
  • Logic and problem-solving
  • Visual and creative math strategies
  • Introductory competition-style problems
02

Intermediate Math

This class is designed for middle school students who want to build stronger reasoning skills and prepare for more challenging mathematical problems.

  • Algebraic thinking
  • Geometry and spatial reasoning
  • Counting, probability, and logic
  • Math competition preparation

How placement works

To make sure every student is in the right class, GroupSolve will use a diagnostic exam before classes begin.

1

Diagnostic exam

Students will complete a short diagnostic exam so we can understand their current strengths, problem-solving style, and readiness level.

2

Level recommendation

Based on the diagnostic, students will be recommended for either the introductory class or the intermediate class.

3

Supportive learning

Students will be encouraged to ask questions, explain their thinking, and learn from both instructors and classmates.

What makes these classes different?

Many math classes focus on giving students formulas and asking them to repeat steps. GroupSolve focuses on helping students discover the ideas behind the formulas.

Instead of only asking, “What is the answer?” we also ask, “Why does this work?” and “How could we solve this another way?”

Class format

Classes will include guided lessons, practice problems, group discussions, and challenge questions. Students will work through problems that help them think independently while still receiving support when they need it.
